a. Generating the data

In this problem the data are (X,Y)(\mathbf{X}, Y)(X,Y) where X=(X1,X2)\mathbf{X}=(X_1, X_2)X=(X1​,X2​) and

  • X1∼Uniform(0,1)X_1 \sim \text{Uniform}(0,1)X1​∼Uniform(0,1)
  • X2=12X2+1100ZX_2 = \frac{1}{2}X_2 + \frac{1}{100}ZX2​=21​X2​+1001​Z where Z∼Normal(0,1)Z\sim \text{Normal}(0,1)Z∼Normal(0,1)

(β0,β1,β2)=(2,2,310)(\beta_0, \beta_1, \beta_2) = (2, 2, \frac{3}{10})(β0​,β1​,β2​)=(2,2,103​)

f. Do these results contradict each other?

Given that the authors want us to consider the correlation between the predictors, I think the intent of this question is to encourage us to wonder why, if X1,X2X_1, X_2X1​,X2​ are so strongly correlated, did the X1X_1X1​ model do such a great job of estimating β1\beta_1β1​ while the X2X_2X2​ model did a poor job of estimating β2\beta_2β2​?

It’s not clear how to answer this question.
